## Product Overview

**One-liner:** Soma is a lunar-cycle fasting and meditation app that translates 1,000+ years of Himalayan practice into a science-backed protocol for mental performance.

**What it does:** Soma tells you when to fast, when to meditate, and how long — aligned to the moon's phases the way Newa Buddhist and Vedic traditions have done for a millennium. It logs your subjective focus, mood, sleep, and HRV (via Apple Health / Whoop / Oura) so you can see whether the protocol actually changes your cognition over time.

**Product category:** Wellness / cognitive performance app. The "shelf" is somewhere between *intermittent fasting trackers* (Zero, MyFast) and *meditation apps* (Calm, Headspace), with a credibility frame closer to *biohacker tools* (Whoop, Oura) than to lunar-wellness apps (Moonly).

**Product type:** Consumer mobile app (iOS-first), freemium SaaS subscription.

**Business model:**
- **Phase 1 (months 0–6):** Free closed beta, ~200 users. No revenue. Goal: adherence data + qualitative interviews.
- **Phase 2 (months 6–12):** Subscription. $12/mo or ~$99/yr. Free tier permanent (free users are the dataset).
- **Phase 3 (months 12–24):** Anonymized longitudinal dataset licensed to research institutions (the real margin play).
- **Phase 4 (months 18+):** Cohort-based courses ($200–500), founder-led.
- **Phase 5 (months 24+):** Physical products (electrolytes, ritual kits) + B2B licensing of the Soma Protocol.

## Problems & Pain Points

**Core problem:** *"My health practice has plateaued and lost meaning. Intermittent fasting works but feels arbitrary. Meditation apps feel saccharine. Biohacker tools track everything but prescribe nothing. I want a protocol that is rigorous, meaningful, and measurable — and I haven't found it."*

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- **Zero / MyFast / Fastic** — pure trackers. No protocol, no meaning, no ritual. Boring after 3 months.
- **Calm / Headspace** — calm down, sleep better. Don't address performance. Don't connect to fasting.
- **Mindy Pelz ecosystem** — single-author, women-and-hormones-only, lacks source-tradition authority.
- **Moonly / Co-Star / The Pattern** — astrology-coded, no scientific posture, no rigor.
- **Generic biohacker stack (supplements, CGM, cold plunge)** — measurement without prescription. No structure.
- **Religious fasting (Lent, Ramadan, Yom Kippur)** — calendar-based but not aligned to lunar cognition cycles.

**What it costs them:**
- Wasted spend on subscriptions they churn out of after 3 months.
- Wasted protocols that don't compound.
- A vague sense that their wellness practice is performative, not transformative.
- For Optimized Skeptics specifically: opportunity cost on cognitive performance they can't quite reach.

**Emotional tension:**
- *"Am I just adding another app, or is this actually different?"*
- *"Will I look ridiculous if I tell my friends I fast by the moon?"*
- *"I want meaning but I'm allergic to woo. Where is that product?"*

## Competitive Landscape

**Direct competitors (lunar-coded fasting):**
- **Moonly** — moon phase + ritual app, but astrology-flavored, no protocol rigor, no fasting depth, no source authority. Falls short because it has no scientific posture and no founder credibility.
- **Mindy Pelz ecosystem (Fast Like a Girl, etc.)** — owns women's lunar/hormonal fasting. Single-author. Falls short on cognition framing, men, and source-tradition authority.

**Secondary competitors (different solution, same problem):**
- **Zero / MyFast / Fastic** — IF tracking. Falls short because it's pure utility with no meaning, no community, no protocol depth.
- **Calm / Headspace** — meditation. Falls short because it doesn't touch fasting, cognition, or ritual.
- **Whoop / Oura / 8Sleep** — biometrics. Falls short because they measure but don't prescribe. Soma reframes them as *instruments* rather than competitors.
- **Huberman / Attia content** — they're upstream demand, not competitors. We route their listeners into Soma.

**Indirect competitors (conflicting approach):**
- **Religious fasting traditions** — already meet the meaning JTBD for some users. Cross-traffic, not competition.
- **The "just buy a moon calendar and DIY it" alternative** — real, but loses on structure, community, and measurement.
- **Doing nothing / cycling through wellness fads** — the realistic default. The biggest competitor of all.

## Differentiation

**Key differentiators:**
1. **Founder source authority** — built by Nepali founders with lived access to Newa Buddhist and Vedic Sanatan Dharma lunar practice. Not a Western repackage. Hardest-to-copy moat in the category.
2. **The data moat** — instrumented from day one for longitudinal cognition + biometric data. Designed to become licensable to research institutions. No competitor in the lunar-wellness category has this posture.
3. **Mental performance framing** — gender-neutral, cognition-first. Sidesteps Mindy Pelz entirely.
4. **The disciplined middle** — between IF apps (rigor, no meaning) and lunar wellness (meaning, no rigor). Owns the gap.
5. **Owned vocabulary** — "Soma Day", "Lunar Chronotype", "Weak-Moon Days", "Chandrayana Mode", "The Soma Protocol". Semantic moat.
6. **Safety as a brand asset** — Chandrayana Mode is gated, pregnancy/ED/T1D are honestly excluded. Most wellness apps hide their disclaimers; Soma celebrates them.

**How we do it differently:**
- We translate Sanskrit and Newa terms inline. They are the second layer ("Why this day?"), never a barrier.
- Default UX is one sentence: *"Tomorrow is a Soma Day. 24-hour fast starts at sunset. Here's your 10-minute meditation."*
- Every fast logs subjective + biometric data, with consent and de-identification designed for research from day one.
- The founder is the on-camera voice of the brand — not a hired teacher, not a Western marketer.

**Why that's better:**
- For the Optimized Skeptic: the rigor is real, the citation is real, the data is real. They will not feel embarrassed recommending it.
- For the Lunar-Curious Native: the tradition is real and grounded, not performed. They get depth without astrology.
- For the Diaspora user: it's theirs.

**Why customers choose us:**
> *"Because the people who built this actually know what they're talking about — and they have the data to prove it."*

## Objections

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| *"This sounds like astrology with extra steps."* | "It's the opposite. We cite Cajochen 2013 on full-moon melatonin disruption, we log HRV against your fasts, and we publish aggregate data. The tradition is real, but the protocol is judged by measurement, not belief." |
| *"I already do 16:8. Why do I need another fasting app?"* | "16:8 is a window. Soma is a calendar. The lunar-aligned protocol gives you variation, meaning, and a framework that compounds — and our data tracks whether it actually moves your HRV and focus." |
| *"Is this safe? Fasting can be dangerous."* | "We have hard safety gates. Pregnancy, eating disorder history, type 1 diabetes, and certain medications are excluded from advanced modes. Chandrayana Mode is gated behind an explicit safety check. We take this seriously enough to say no to users." |
| *"Isn't this cultural appropriation?"* | "Soma is built by Nepali founders from the source tradition, with diaspora advisors on every culturally-loaded decision. We're not borrowing this — we're translating it." |
| *"I don't want to learn Sanskrit to use an app."* | "You won't have to. Default UX is one sentence: 'Tomorrow is a Soma Day. 24-hour fast starts at sunset.' Sanskrit is available as a second layer if you want depth." |
| *"What if I have a birthday dinner during a fast day?"* | "Shift the fast by ±1 day with one tap. We respect real life. The app shows you the trade-off so you stay informed, not boxed in." |

**Anti-personas (do not market to):**
- Pure weight-loss IF users.
- Hardcore astrology audiences.
- Spiritual-bypass / "high-vibe" wellness influencers.
- People with active eating disorders.
- People looking for medical treatment.
- Anyone whose primary search query is "how to lose 10 lbs fast".

## Switching Dynamics (JTBD Four Forces)

**Push** *(what frustrates them about today)*:
- IF feels arbitrary and boring after the first year.
- Meditation apps don't connect to anything else they do.
- Biohacker tools measure everything but tell them nothing.
- Their wellness routine has lost meaning and they feel performative.
- They've started to suspect Mindy Pelz's framework is too narrow / single-author.

**Pull** *(what attracts them to Soma)*:
- A 1,000-year-old protocol with founder authority no Western brand has.
- Measurable cognitive performance instrumented from day one.
- A calendar-driven rhythm that creates anticipation, not just compliance.
- Cohort fasts — community without influencer culture.
- The promise of being part of the first real longitudinal dataset on lunar-aligned fasting.

**Habit** *(what keeps them stuck with the current approach)*:
- The Zero/MyFast streak counter — sunk-cost emotional weight.
- Existing Apple Health/Whoop integration inertia.
- Identity attachment to "I do 16:8" as part of who they are.
- Fear that switching means starting over.

**Anxiety** *(what worries them about switching)*:
- *"What if it's just dressed-up moon astrology?"*
- *"What if I miss a fast and the app shames me?"*
- *"What if I have to learn a bunch of Sanskrit to feel like I belong?"*
- *"What if my friends think this is weird?"*
- *"What if it's not safe for me?"*

**Implication:** Onboarding must defuse all four anxieties in the first 3 minutes, AND respect the existing habit by importing from Apple Health/Zero on day one.

## Brand Voice

**Tone:** Calm, certain, intellectually rigorous, warm but never saccharine. The voice of a teacher who doesn't need to raise their hand.

**Style:**
- **Founder voice (first-person):** Personal, lineage-aware, occasional untranslated Sanskrit/Newa with inline gloss. Used for any cultural or spiritual framing. Always written or spoken by the founder.
- **Brand voice (third-person):** Restrained, evidence-led, specific. Used for product copy, science framing, and PR. Written by marketing, but always checked against the claims grid.
- The two voices alternate and never blur. We do not let marketing speak for the tradition.

**Personality (5 adjectives, in priority):**
1. **Rooted** — every claim has a tradition or a study behind it.
2. **Precise** — *manas*, not "vibes". *24-hour fast at sunset*, not "fast for a while".
3. **Quiet** — the confidence of something old. We do not shout.
4. **Modern** — typography, UX, and tone are unmistakably 2026.
5. **Honest** — we tell users what we don't know.

**Archetype:** Sage (primary) + Magician (secondary). Explicitly NOT Hero (hustle) or Lover (aesthetic spirituality).
