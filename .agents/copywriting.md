## 1. Waitlist Landing Page

**Page goal:** One action — join the waitlist (email capture).
**Traffic:** Founder Substack, X threads, diaspora networks, early podcast mentions.
**Prior knowledge of visitor:** Likely knows the brand exists and is curious. Likely skeptical by default (P1). Will leave if the page sounds spiritual.

---

### Above the fold

**Headline (option A — recommended)**
> **Fast and meditate with the moon. The way it's been done for a thousand years.**

**Option B**
> **Moon for Mental Performance.**

**Option C**
> **The mind has a calendar. We finally found it.**

*Rationale: A is the recommended headline for the waitlist page because it's the most concrete and least reliant on prior brand awareness. It describes what you do in 12 words and anchors the claim to tradition without using the word "ancient". B is the brand tagline and works better as the page title / browser tab and as the footer signature. C is the strongest headline for the founder essay and the launch-day press release — save it.*

**Subheadline**
> A lunar-cycle fasting and meditation protocol, translated from Newa Buddhist and Vedic tradition into software your phone and your wearable understand. Built by founders from Nepal. Instrumented from day one to measure whether it actually works.

*Annotation: three ideas in one subhead — tradition, modernity, measurement. Exactly the three pillars from the positioning doc. "Instrumented" is the Huberman-audience word. No word from the "avoid" list.*

**Primary CTA**
> **Join the waitlist** → *(email field)*
> *Button text:* **Claim my spot**

*Alt CTAs tested:*
- *"Get on the list" — too weak*
- *"Sign up for early access" — generic*
- *"Claim my spot" — possessive, active, implies scarcity without saying it*

**Trust microcopy under the CTA**
> We are taking 200 founding members. No spam. One email every new moon.

*Annotation: scarcity, promise, and a lunar-framing flourish in 12 words. "Every new moon" is on-brand and more memorable than "monthly".*

*Annotation: this is where the lunar/tradition reveal lands — AFTER the skeptic has been validated on their actual pain. ### Section 4 — The "what you get" section (what the app actually does)

**Section header**
> **What happens when you join.**

**Three-column layout or stacked cards:**

**1. A calendar you don't have to think about.**
Every day the app tells you one thing: is today a Soma Day or not. If it is, you fast from sunset to sunset and meditate for 10 minutes. If it isn't, you rest. You don't have to learn Sanskrit to use it. (You can if you want to.)

**2. Measurement from day one.**
Soma reads from Apple Health, Whoop, and Oura. Every fast logs your subjective focus, mood, and sleep alongside your HRV and glucose. At the end of your first lunar cycle, you get a personal report — your Lunar Chronotype — showing how *your* body responds to the protocol. Not anyone else's.

**3. A community that fasts together.**
Every full moon, hundreds of Soma members fast together across five continents. The cohort fast is free, optional, and the only "streak" we honor. No shaming, no punishment for missing one, no guilt.

*Annotation: each card is one idea (Haines rule). Card 2 is the headline differentiator and should be the tallest column if the layout allows. Card 3 defuses the "streak anxiety" objection from Theme 4.*

### Section 5 — The objection handler (FAQ-shaped but not labeled FAQ)

**Section header**
> **Honest answers to honest questions.**

**Q: This sounds like astrology.**
It's the opposite. Every claim we make cites a study. Every study we cite is named. The lunar framework is the oldest continuous human fasting protocol — but we're judging it by your HRV and your focus, not by belief.

**Q: Is it safe?**
For most people, yes. For some people — pregnancy, a history of eating disorders, type 1 diabetes, certain medications — fasting is not safe, and Soma will tell you that before you start. Our most advanced protocol, *Chandrayana Mode*, is gated behind an explicit safety check. We built the strictest safety system in the category on purpose.

**Q: Do I have to learn Sanskrit?**
No. The default app experience is one sentence a day. The tradition is available as a second layer for users who want depth.

**Q: What if a fast day falls on my daughter's birthday?**
Shift it. The app respects real life. One tap moves the fast ±1 day and shows you the trade-off honestly. We're not running a discipline cult.

**Q: How much does it cost?**
The beta is free. The paid tier launches later this year at $149/year for Soma Plus, with a permanent free tier and a capped *Founding 500* offer at $79/year for life.

**Q: Why should I trust you?**
Because we are Nepali founders building this in the open, with a scientific advisor, a published claims policy, and a research dataset that will be peer-reviewed. Read the founder essay. Judge us on that.

*Annotation: six questions, in descending order of skeptic-first objections, ending with the trust objection. Every answer is short. No answer evades.*

### FAQ

**Can I cancel anytime?**
Yes. Cancellation doesn't delete your data or your account — it just downgrades you to Free. Your streaks, reports, and adherence history stay with you.

**What if I need to pause?**
You can pause for up to 3 lunar cycles without losing anything. Travel, illness, pregnancy, life — we built this to survive real life.

**Is there a refund policy?**
30-day full refund on annual plans, no questions asked. If Soma isn't for you, we'd rather refund you than keep you.

**What about religious fasting traditions like Ramadan or Lent?**
Soma is not a substitute for religious observance, and we don't claim to be. Several of our members also observe Ramadan, Lent, and Yom Kippur — Soma pauses automatically during those windows if you opt in.

**How do you use my data?**
We collect fast adherence, subjective reports, and biometric data. Your data is de-identified, aggregated, and — only with your explicit opt-in — contributed to research. We do not sell individual data. Ever. Full policy: [link].

**What is Chandrayana Mode?**
A graduated fasting protocol from the Vedic tradition — 15 mouthfuls on the full moon, decreasing by one per day to water-only on the new moon, then rebuilding. It is behind a medical safety gate and not available to users with certain conditions. Read the protocol explainer before enabling.

## 5. What's NOT included here (next copywriting tasks, not for this round)

1. **The founder essay itself** (the ~3,000-word cornerstone) — this is a founder-written asset that should not be ghostwritten. The marketing team provides the outline and the essay structure; the founder writes it. First draft is the #1 pre-launch dependency.
2. **Email sequence copy** — welcome → cohort fast invite → beta onboarding → first data drop. Use the `email-sequence` skill next.
3. **In-app onboarding copy** — the first-run screens, the Lunar Chronotype baseline flow, the Chandrayana Mode safety gate. Use the `onboarding-cro` skill.
4. **Press kit boilerplate** — the 150-word standard company description that every journalist will copy-paste. Should be drafted in the Wave-1 phase.
5. **App Store listing** — name, subtitle, description, screenshots, preview video copy. Phase 4 task.
