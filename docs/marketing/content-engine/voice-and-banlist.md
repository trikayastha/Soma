# Voice DNA + Banlist — Soma

> The quality gate every drop clears before publish. Ported from Yirifi's `blog-voice-dna.md` + brand banlist; rewritten for Soma's register. Editor scores each asset "would Soma actually say this?" — pass / revise / reject.

---

## The register

Soma sounds like the landing page: **quiet, reverent, plain, and warm.** It carries something ancient without cosplaying a guru. Think of a knowledgeable friend who grew up with the tradition and explains it without a trace of superiority.

**Five voice attributes:**
1. **Reverent, not preachy.** Honor the tradition; never lecture or gate-keep. "Anyone, anywhere, can keep the rhythm."
2. **Plain over mystical.** Explain a tithi like you'd explain it to a friend. Poetry is earned in small doses ("fast with the moon"), not sprayed everywhere.
3. **Calm and unhurried.** No urgency, no FOMO, no streak-anxiety. The moon isn't in a rush.
4. **Grounded in specifics.** Name the fast, the day, the rule. Concrete beats vague ("the 11th lunar day, without grains" > "a sacred time of purification").
5. **Honest about the beta.** Building in the open. "It's still early. Take a look." No overclaiming.

**Devanagari rule:** always pair Sanskrit with a gloss on first use — एकादशी *(Ekadashi)*. It's a texture, not a test.

**Rhythm:** short sentences. Let one land before the next. The site's em-dashes and gentle cadence are the house style.

## Banlist (reject on sight)

**Wellness-hustle clichés:** detox, cleanse (as in "cleanse your toxins"), biohack, "reset your gut", "burn fat", "shed pounds", "bikini body", grind, "no excuses", "crush your goals", "level up your wellness".

**Medical / health claims:** any promise of weight loss, disease cure, metabolic outcome, or "scientifically proven" benefit. Soma is a spiritual + rhythm practice, **not** a health intervention — never imply medical benefit. (Legal + trust risk.)

**Guru / spiritual-bypass:** "manifest", "high vibration", "align your chakras" (unless literally accurate and sourced), "the universe wants you to…", "sacred feminine energy", generic pan-spiritual mush that flattens the specific Hindu tradition into wellness wallpaper.

**Guilt / streak-anxiety:** "don't break your streak", "cheat day", "you failed your fast", "should", shame framing of any kind.

**Corporate filler:** "we're excited to announce", "thrilled to share", "game-changer", "revolutionary", "seamless", "in this post we'll explore".

**Appropriation risk:** never present the tradition as invented-here or as a generic "moon ritual" stripped of its Hindu roots. Credit *Sanatan Dharma* explicitly.

## Editor checklist (every drop)

- [ ] Reads like the landing page — quiet, plain, warm
- [ ] One clear takeaway; not a wall of text
- [ ] Every claim grounded in tradition, not invented
- [ ] Sanskrit glossed on first use
- [ ] No banlist words
- [ ] No medical/weight-loss claim
- [ ] No guilt / streak framing
- [ ] Tradition credited to its Hindu roots
- [ ] CTA is the app as *practical* payoff, not a hard sell
- [ ] `primary_engagement_signal` set + its mechanic present

**Voice test:** read it aloud. If it sounds like a supplement ad, a yoga influencer, or a SaaS changelog — reject and rewrite.
