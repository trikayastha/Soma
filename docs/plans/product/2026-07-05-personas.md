## How these were derived

Soma segments its users through the data it collects, not through demographics. The
strongest signal is the **intent picker** (`onboarding/IntentRouter.tsx`), which maps
each answer to a starting **theme** and **voice** — so intent effectively chooses the
personality of the whole app. The personas below are built on that mapping, then
sharpened with the **goal** field (focus / calm / discipline / metabolic), **experience**
level (none / some / experienced), and the **tradition-vs-science copy split** in
`lib/whyThisDay.ts` (every fast day carries a `plain`, a `tradition`, and a `science`
paragraph — different readers open different ones).

| Intent | → Theme | → Voice | Persona |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| `optimize` | performance | scientific | The Optimizer |
| `tradition` | devotional | traditional | The Tradition-Keeper |
| `tired` | minimal | coach | The App-Fatigued Minimalist |
| `curious` | performance | coach | The Curious Newcomer |

A fifth section documents who Soma **deliberately turns away** via the safety gate.

## 1. The Optimizer — "show me the mechanism"

**Snapshot.** Picks *Optimize my body* (performance theme, scientific voice). Goal is
`focus` or `metabolic`. Experience `some` or `experienced` — already runs 16:8, may have
done 24h. Treats lunar timing as a structured schedule that removes the daily "should I
fast today?" decision, not as devotion.

**Goals.** Metabolic flexibility, sharper deep-work windows, a repeatable cadence they
don't have to negotiate with themselves each morning.

**Behaviors.**
- Reads the **Science** disclosure under "Why this day?" (the Cajochen 2013 sleep study,
  the "predictable 14-day rhythm trains metabolic flexibility" framing) and skips
  Tradition.
- Actually fills in **before/after subjective logs** (energy, focus, mood, sleep) and
  watches **Personal Deltas** accumulate.
- Cares about the **mandala completion rate** as a performance readout — but Soma
  refuses to gamify it into a streak (`__tests__/no-streak.test.ts`), which they either
  respect or find under-motivating.

**Frustrations.**
- Wants proof, not vibes. If deltas need too many logged sessions before they surface,
  they lose interest.
- Suspicious of anything that reads as mystical; the wellness-not-medicine disclaimer
  reassures them the app isn't overclaiming.

**Lives in.** Today (deltas, Science copy) · Rhythm (mandala as a metric) · Wisdom
(Science reads).

**What consolidation changes for them.** The **experience + intensity merge** in
onboarding removes a redundant hop — they already know they want 16h, so asking
experience *and then* intensity felt like the app doubting them. The integrated **Month
calendar inside Today** means their schedule and their day-detail live in one place
instead of losing the selection when they tab away (audit F1).

## 2. The Tradition-Keeper — "this is older than any app"

**Snapshot.** Picks *Follow tradition* (devotional theme, traditional voice). Goal is
usually `discipline` (Ritual & discipline: "meaningful self-care rhythm"). Has lineage
to the practice — grew up around Ekadashi or Purnima observance, or is reconnecting with
it. Values that the founders have "lived lineage to the source tradition" (Settings
footer).

**Goals.** Keep the vrat properly and consistently; honour the observance, not optimise a
biomarker. Correct timing matters — sunrise-anchored tithi, the right parana window.

**Behaviors.**
- Reads the **Tradition** disclosure (Sanatan Dharma framing, Newa Buddhist "weak-moon"
  teaching, the Skanda / Ganesha / Ishana Samhita references).
- Sets a **location** so tithi is anchored to local sunrise and the **Ekadashi name**
  (Putrada, Vaikuntha) and **parana break-fast window** appear.
- Observes the fuller set of days — Pradosh, Sankashti Chaturthi, Shivaratri — not just
  the headline Ekadashi/Purnima.

**Frustrations.**
- Approximate or noon-UTC tithi feels wrong; they want the `ComputedAtBanner` to say
  "sunrise," which means location must be easy to set.
- Any copy that flattens the tradition into a generic "wellness hack" reads as
  disrespectful.

**Lives in.** Today (Ekadashi name, parana, Tradition copy) · Wisdom (Tradition reads) ·
Settings (location, devotional theme, traditional voice).

**What consolidation changes for them.** **Location moves out of the required onboarding
path** — a risk for this persona, since accurate tithi depends on it. The mitigation
(audit F3): the `ComputedAtBanner` prompts for location later with an accuracy note, so
they can still reach sunrise-anchored timing without a forced step. Worth watching in
metrics: do tradition-intent users set location less often post-consolidation?

## 3. The App-Fatigued Minimalist — "less choice, more rhythm"

**Snapshot.** Picks *Tired of fasting apps* (minimal theme, coach voice). Goal `calm` or
`discipline`. Has churned out of streak-driven, notification-heavy fasting apps and
explicitly wants fewer decisions. The product's own carousel speaks directly to them:
"A rhythm, not a regimen… chosen by tradition, not by your willpower."

**Goals.** Show up on the handful of days the moon dictates, do the fast, close the app.
No dashboards to tend, no streak to protect, no guilt.

**Behaviors.**
- Uses **Today** almost exclusively. Rarely opens Wisdom or drills into Tradition/Science.
- Keeps notifications **quiet** (the default `notificationPhilosophy`).
- Values that a missed day never shows as "broken" — the mandala stays *in-progress* or
  *partial*, never punishing (`MandalaStatus` never `'broken'`).

**Frustrations.**
- Every extra onboarding step, settings section, or tab is friction that reminds them of
  the apps they left.
- Any hint of streak mechanics or loss-aversion nudging would break trust immediately.

**Lives in.** Today, and little else by choice.

**What consolidation changes for them.** This is the persona the whole consolidation
serves most. **Onboarding 8 → ~5 steps**, **nav 5 → 4 tabs**, and **Settings 9 → 6
sections** all reduce exactly the surface area they resent. The deferred archetype quiz
(now optional in Settings, audit F3) means they're never quizzed before their first fast.

## 4. The Curious Newcomer — "what's this moon thing?"

**Snapshot.** Picks *Curious about the moon* (performance theme, coach voice). Experience
`none` — new to fasting entirely. Goal often `calm`. Came for the aesthetic and the idea;
doesn't yet know Ekadashi from Amavasya.

**Goals.** Try it gently, understand what's happening, not hurt themselves.

**Behaviors.**
- Reads the **plain** copy (the neutral top paragraph of "Why this day?"), not yet the
  Tradition/Science disclosures.
- Guided toward **12h** intensity by the experience → intensity suggestion.
- Leans on the coach voice and the reassuring rest-day copy ("not every day has to be a
  fast day").

**Frustrations.**
- Can be overwhelmed by Sanskrit or unexplained jargon — the product deliberately keeps
  Sanskrit out of body copy and archetype labels (wind/fire/earth instead of
  Vata/Pitta/Kapha) to protect this persona.
- Needs the safety framing to feel careful, not scary.

**Lives in.** Today (plain copy, gentle guidance) · Wisdom Learn section (short, honest,
optional) · the onboarding carousel.

**What consolidation changes for them.** **Intent + goal merge** and **experience +
intensity merge** shorten the path to first value, which matters most for someone with no
prior commitment. Risk to watch: the newcomer benefits most from the welcome carousel's
framing — if the merge trims that context too aggressively, first-fast activation could
drop. The Learn section staying inside Wisdom (post-merge) keeps their "explain it to me"
content one tab away.

## 5. Who Soma turns away (anti-persona)

Not a user to design *for* — a boundary the product enforces. The onboarding **safety
gate** (`lib/safety.ts`) hard-blocks anyone who flags:

- **Under 18** — "fasting during adolescence can interfere with growth and development."
- **Pregnant or breastfeeding** — not safe; consult a physician.
- **History of disordered eating** — structured fasting can be harmful; work with a
  clinician.
- **Diabetes (type 1 or 2)** — requires medical supervision Soma doesn't provide.

These are honest refusals with reasons, not upsells. They reinforce the
**wellness-not-medicine** positioning that every other persona relies on to trust the
app. Consolidation must **not** move the safety step out of the required flow — unlike
archetype and location, it is load-bearing and stays. (See PSAK guardrail metrics.)
