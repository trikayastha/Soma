# Soma — Product Context

**Tagline:** *Moon for Mental Performance*
**One-liner:** A lunar-cycle fasting and meditation app that turns 1,000+ years of Himalayan practice into a simple, science-backed protocol for Western minds.
**Primary market:** Western wellness, biohacking, and productivity audiences (US/EU-first).
**Founding edge:** Built by founders from Nepal with lived cultural access to Vedic Sanatan Dharma lunar practices — not a repackage of Western wellness trends.

---

## 1. Origin of the Idea

The concept started from a cultural observation: in Nepalese tradition, regular fasting is tied to lunar phases — *Ekadashi*, *Chaturthi*, *Purnima* (full moon), and *Amavasya* (new moon). The question was whether Western culture has a comparable framework, and whether the Eastern practice could be translated into a product Western audiences would actually adopt.

Western parallels exist but are shallow by comparison:
- **Intermittent fasting** — modern, metabolic, secular.
- **Religious fasting** — Lent, Ramadan, Yom Kippur — calendar-based, not lunar-aligned in the precise way Vedic practice is.
- **Moon-cycle wellness** — exists in the influencer fringe, but lacks a rigorous, traditional backbone.

Soma fills the gap between "intermittent fasting app" and "woo-woo moon ritual" — a disciplined middle that is both culturally authentic and scientifically legible.

## 2. Cultural Credibility Layer (Founder Moat)

This is Soma's hardest-to-copy asset and should be front-and-center in positioning.

### Newa Buddhism (Kathmandu Valley)
- **Dashami Puja** and **Chhare Puja** are tied to specific lunar phases.
- The underlying teaching: when the moon is in certain phases, the mind is *mentally weaker / more reactive*, and ritual practice (fasting, meditation, puja) is needed as a stabilizer.
- The moon is explicitly understood as a **mirror of the mental state**, not a metaphor.

### Sanatan Dharma / Vedic Tradition
- **Ekadashi** (11th lunar day) fasting, observed across ~6 days per lunar month.
- **Chandrayana Vrat** — a graduated fast: 15 mouthfuls at full moon, decreasing by one per day to water-only at new moon, then rebuilding. A fully-engineered lunar protocol from ~1,000+ years ago.
- Yogic framing: moon governs *manas* (mind/emotion); fasting on shift-days purifies the *Antahkarana* (mind, intellect, ego, consciousness).

### Why this matters for Western positioning
Western wellness brands are *copying* lunar fasting from a distance. Soma's founders have direct, lived access to the source tradition. That is not marketing — it is a credibility asset. The positioning should be:

> "Soma is built by people whose grandparents fasted by the moon. We are not selling you a trend. We are translating a 1,000-year-old operating system for the mind into something your phone understands."

## 3. Target Audience (Western)

**Primary persona — "The Optimized Skeptic"**
- 28–45, urban, earns $80k+, already uses Calm/Headspace/Apple Health/Whoop.
- Has tried 16:8 intermittent fasting. It worked, then got boring.
- Reads Huberman, follows Peter Attia, curious about Ayurveda but suspicious of anything that sounds vague.
- Wants **measurable cognitive performance**, not spiritual aesthetics.
- Will pay for proof and ritual — not for affirmations.

**Secondary persona — "The Lunar-Curious Wellness Native"**
- 25–40, predominantly female, into cycle-syncing, Dr. Mindy Pelz's work, astrology-adjacent but not astrology-defined.
- Already believes the moon affects her. Wants structure and community.

**Who we are NOT targeting (initially):**
- Hardcore spiritual practitioners (they don't need an app).
- Generic IF users who only care about weight loss.
- Pure astrology audiences (credibility dilution).

## 5. Product Principles (User-Centric, Ingenuity-First)

The red thread from the customer-simulation section: **simplicity, proof, credibility, life-integration**. Every feature must pass all four.

### 5.1 Radical simplicity over Sanskrit literacy
- Users should **never** need to learn tithis, Chandrayana mechanics, or moon-day names to use Soma.
- Default interface: "Tomorrow is a Soma Day. 24-hour fast starts at sunset. Here's your 10-minute meditation."
- Sanskrit and tradition are available as a **second layer** ("Why this day?") for users who want depth — not as a barrier.

### 5.2 The Data Moat (the real business)
The monetizable asset is **longitudinal self-reported + biometric data** on how lunar-aligned fasting/meditation affects cognition, sleep, mood, glucose, HRV.

- Every fast logs: subjective energy, focus, mood, sleep quality, HRV (via Apple Health / Whoop / Oura integration), optional CGM.
- Aggregated, anonymized, consented dataset becomes licensable to research institutions (Stanford, Huberman Lab, academic sleep/metabolic labs).
- This is the Strava-for-wellness play: the app is free/cheap; the **dataset is the business**.
- **Consent, de-identification, and IRB-ready protocols must be designed from day one**, not retrofitted. This is both an ethical and a commercial requirement.

### 5.3 Ingenuity in app design — ideas to explore
Each should be stress-tested for both upside and downside.

| Idea | Upside | Downside / Risk |
|---|---|---|
| **Moon-phase home screen** that physically mirrors the current lunar phase in real time, with your fast window overlaid | Emotional anchor, daily re-engagement, beautiful | Gimmicky if not tied to data; easy to copy visually |
| **"Lunar Chronotype" onboarding** — 7-day baseline where the app learns YOUR response to phases before prescribing anything | Personalization, defensible data, trust-building | Slower time-to-value; user may churn before seeing benefit |
| **Graduated Chandrayana mode** (advanced) — the 15→1 mouthful protocol, translated into modern calorie/meal targets | Unique, zero competitors have this, deeply authentic | Medical risk if unsupervised; must have hard safety rails |
| **Meditation that shortens on fast days, deepens on feed days** | Fasting + meditation actually sync instead of being bolted together | Requires original audio content — expensive |
| **"Weak-moon" reframe** — Newa Buddhist teaching that some phases require *more* mental support, not less | Differentiated, scientifically congruent (full-moon sleep disruption) | Spiritual language may alienate pure-biohacker segment |
| **Cohort fasting** — everyone on Soma fasts together on Ekadashi/full moon | Community, adherence, virality ("tonight we fast") | Logistical — time zones, cultural holidays, opt-out handling |
| **Flex rule engine** — "daughter's birthday tomorrow? Shift the fast by one day, here's the trade-off" | Respects real life, addresses the top customer-simulation objection | Complex to build; undermines discipline if overused |
| **Founder face** — short weekly video from a Nepali founder / teacher contextualizing the coming lunar week | Solves the credibility gap identified in customer sim | Creates key-person dependency; must be sustainable |
| **Research-grade export** — users can download their own data in a format labs accept | Trust, science-aligned brand, regulatory clarity | Invites scrutiny; data must actually be clean |

### 5.4 Integration with real life (the dealbreaker)
The customer simulation was explicit: rigid rules kill adoption. The app must let users:
- Shift a fast by ±1 day with a visible "tradition vs. life" explainer.
- Scale intensity (12h / 16h / 24h / Chandrayana).
- Pause for illness, travel, pregnancy, medication — with explicit safety gates.

## 6. Honest Criticism — What Must Be Solved

These are the hard truths surfaced in the "criticize harshly" pass. They are load-bearing and should stay visible in every planning doc.

1. **Market saturation is real.** Moonly, Dr. Mindy Pelz, cycle-syncing apps, every IF tracker. Soma's differentiator is *not* "fasting by the moon" — that exists. It is *authentic source + rigorous data capture + mental-performance framing*. If any of those three weakens, Soma becomes a me-too.

2. **The Western science for lunar effects is thin.** The Calcutta diabetic study is n=42. The Cajochen sleep study is real but narrow. Soma must be **careful with claims**: wellness claims, not medical claims, until the in-app dataset itself generates real evidence. Regulatory line must be explicit from day one (FTC, FDA wellness-vs-medical, GDPR/CCPA for data).

3. **Mindy Pelz owns women's lunar fasting in the West.** Soma cannot fight her on her turf. Either complement (mental performance, gender-neutral cognition framing) or differentiate (Newa Buddhist source authority she does not have).

4. **Cultural translation is harder than it looks.** Ekadashi works in Nepal because grandparents did it. In the West there is no scaffolding. Soma must build that scaffolding inside the app — micro-education, ritual cues, community — without it feeling like a religion class.

5. **Founder is in Nepal — distribution disadvantage.** Paid acquisition in US wellness is brutally expensive. The counter-move is earned credibility: podcasts (Huberman-adjacent, Peter Attia–adjacent, Rich Roll), long-form content, and the founder-as-teacher angle. Paid ads should be a *late* lever, not an early one.

6. **Revenue-model discipline.** App subscription + course + community + physical products + B2B licensing *cannot* all ship at once. Sequencing must be:
   1. Free/cheap app → harvest data + adherence signal.
   2. Premium tier (guided programs, deeper meditation library, cohort fasts).
   3. Research partnerships and licensed dataset (the real margin).
   4. Courses / cohort programs once teacher brand is established.
   5. Physical products (teas, electrolytes, ritual kits) — only after brand is earned.

7. **Validation gap.** No product decisions before ~10–20 real customer conversations, a waitlist, and a small (50-person) beta. Build the minimum needed to test whether people will actually fast on a schedule we give them.

## 7. Monetization Strategy (Sequenced)

**Phase 1 — Earn the right to exist (months 0–6)**
- Closed beta, free. 50–200 users.
- Goal: adherence data, qualitative interviews, founder content library.
- Revenue: zero. This phase is data and trust.

**Phase 2 — Subscription (months 6–12)**
- $12–15/mo or ~$99/yr. Premium unlocks: full meditation library, Chandrayana mode, cohort fasts, deeper analytics.
- Free tier stays, because free users are the dataset.

**Phase 3 — Research licensing (months 12–24)**
- Structured, IRB-aware dataset offered to academic labs (Stanford, Huberman, sleep/metabolic research groups).
- Revenue: licensing fees, co-authored studies (which in turn feed marketing credibility — a flywheel).

**Phase 4 — Teacher-led programs (months 18+)**
- Cohort-based courses, $200–500. Founder-led. Limited seats to preserve authenticity.

**Phase 5 — Physical + B2B (months 24+)**
- Ritual kits, lunar-phase electrolyte/tea line.
- B2B licensing of the Soma protocol to meditation apps, retreats, corporate wellness.

**The real money play is Phase 3.** The app is the data collection instrument.

## 8. Open Questions

These need answers before building anything meaningful:

1. Who is the face of Soma? (Founder? Nepali teacher? Hybrid?)
2. Male + female, or launch gender-neutral "mental performance" angle first and layer cycle-syncing later?
3. What is the single most important metric for Phase 1 adherence — fasts completed, subjective focus delta, or HRV delta?
4. Which wearable integration is non-negotiable at launch? (Apple Health is minimum; Whoop/Oura are leverage.)
5. Where does Chandrayana Vrat live — launch feature or advanced unlock? (Safety implications either way.)
6. What is the legal wrapper for the research dataset play — US nonprofit? Data trust? For-profit with consented opt-in?
7. How do we handle the Ramadan / Yom Kippur / Lent audiences who *already* fast religiously — complementary cross-traffic, or distraction?

Soma wins if, 18 months from now, a Stanford sleep researcher publishes a paper using Soma data, and a biohacker on a podcast says *"I fast by the moon now — there's this app built by Nepali founders, it actually works."*

Everything in the roadmap should be judged against that sentence.
