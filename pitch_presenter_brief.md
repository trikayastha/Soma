## 0. How to use this document

- **Section A** is the slide-by-slide talking track timed for 5 minutes (~27 sec/slide on average; the title and vision slides are tighter, the solution and roadmap slides are richer).
- **Section B** is the *adversarial prep* — where judges will press, and the strongest one-line counter for each.
- **Section C** is the *self-check* — internal weak points the presenter should be aware of *before* walking on stage, including what to say if asked something we don't yet know.
- **Section D** is logistical (room setup, fallbacks, what to leave out).

The deck navigates with `→` / `←`, number keys jump to slides, and click also advances. Use a wired clicker if possible.

## A. Slide-by-slide talking track (5:00 total)

> Read this once out loud before pitching. The point is rhythm, not memorization.

### Slide 1 — Cover · "Moon for Mental Performance" *(0:15)*
> "Soma is a lunar-cycle fasting and meditation app. It is built by Nepali founders with direct lineage to a thousand-year-old Himalayan tradition — and it is instrumented for science from day one. In the next five minutes I want to show you the context, the problem, the wedge, and the business behind it."

**Cue:** Wait one beat after the title. The moon visual is doing work.

### Slide 2 — Context · "The mind has a calendar" *(0:30)*
> "For more than a thousand years, Newa Buddhist and Vedic Sanatan Dharma traditions have prescribed fasting on specific lunar days — Ekadashi, full moon, new moon. The yogic framing is that the moon governs *manas*, the mind. The Western world rediscovered intermittent fasting in 2015. The Himalayas engineered a complete lunar protocol around the year 1000."

**Cue:** Point to the timeline. The orange marks are the prescribed Soma Days.

### Slide 3 — Problem · "Self-care has become a content library" *(0:35)*
> "Optimization-minded adults — Whoop owners, Huberman listeners — have plateaued on intermittent fasting and burned out on meditation apps that feel like Netflix for stress. They want meaning *with* measurement. Calm owns calm. Zero owns the timer. Mindy Pelz owns women's hormonal fasting. Moonly owns vibes. Nobody is occupying the quadrant where Soma sits: culturally rooted, scientifically literate, performance-framed."

**Cue:** Three numbers on the right are the quantified pain — $60–180 paid CAC, sub-30% wellness D365, zero longitudinal lunar+cognition datasets in market.

### Slide 4 — Why now · "The category is real, the story is unclaimed" *(0:40)*
> "The demand is verified. Mindy Pelz sold over a million books on lunar fasting. Zero hit ten million downloads. Calm and Headspace clear three hundred million in combined ARR. And wearable penetration in our target persona has crossed thirty percent — biometric integration is now table stakes. The map on the right is the competitive landscape. Soma is the only player in the upper-right: high cultural authenticity *and* high scientific rigor."

**Cue:** This is the "you don't have to create demand" beat. Sell the *capture*, not the creation.

### Slide 5 — Solution · "Fast with the moon. Think with clarity." *(0:50)*
> "The product is one screen. Tomorrow is a Soma Day — twenty-four-hour fast starting at sunset, paired with a ten-minute meditation, with a one-tap *'Why this day?'* explainer for the depth. Three loops: *schedule*, *pair*, *measure*. The lunar engine prescribes about six fasts per cycle. The meditation player lengthens on feed days and shortens on fast days. And every fast logs subjective focus alongside HRV and sleep from Apple Health, Whoop, or Oura — so the protocol is judged by data, not belief."

**Cue:** Hold the phone mockup up with your hand if it helps. Don't read the pill chips — they exist as visual proof of feature depth.

### Slide 6 — Moat · "Two moats, both compounding" *(0:35)*
> "There are two moats and they reinforce each other. The first is *lineage* — built by founders whose grandparents actually did this. Western lunar-wellness brands are tourists. We are native. The second is *data* — IRB-grade consent and an append-only event log from day one. By month eighteen the dataset itself is the most defensible asset in the category. The flywheel: founder credibility brings users, users generate consented data, the data produces published findings, and the findings refresh founder credibility."

**Cue:** Trace the orange arrows on the flywheel with a finger or laser. People remember the visual, not the words.

### Slide 7 — Market · "A defensible niche, not a mass-market app" *(0:25)*
> "We are honest about size. TAM is the eleven-billion-dollar global wellness app market growing seventeen percent. Realistic SOM in year three is three to eight million ARR — under half a percent of the served market. Plus a data-licensing layer of two to ten million a year by years four and five. ARPU ninety-nine, gross margin seventy-five percent, organic CAC under thirty. Six-to-one LTV-to-CAC — but only on the organic path. We are pricing this round to that shape: angel and small seed, not a premature Series A."

**Cue:** This is the slide where you *under-promise on purpose*. Investors trust founders who name their ceiling.

### Slide 8 — Roadmap · "Five gates. No phase begins until the prior one passes." *(0:35)*
> "Five gates. We do not write production code until five hundred people are on the waitlist. We do not ship a paid tier until forty percent of beta users complete a full lunar cycle. We do not open data licensing until subscription is self-sustaining. The revenue layers stack the same way — subscription first, then data licensing, then cohorts and B2B. Each layer earns the right to exist."

**Cue:** Walk the dots left-to-right. The discipline of the gates *is* the pitch.

### Slide 9 — GTM · "Earned, not paid" *(0:30)*
> "US wellness paid CAC is sixty to one hundred eighty dollars and would incinerate runway before adherence is proven. So zero paid spend for the first nine months. The founder is the content engine — weekly long-form essays, podcast guesting on Huberman and Attia adjacent shows, diaspora seeding, and one credentialed scientific advisor on record before launch. Three waves: Whisper, Signal, Compound. Total first-year marketing budget: sixty to ninety-five thousand dollars."

**Cue:** This is where you preempt the "how will Nepali founders crack US wellness" objection. Name the risk before they do.

### Slide 10 — Risks · "We have written them down before you can ask" *(0:25)*
> "Six things could kill us. Distribution from Nepal — mitigated by the founder content engine and one US advisor. Beta adherence below forty percent — hard gate, no spend until proven. Cultural appropriation — founder is the only voice for cultural framing, diaspora advisory reviews every line. FTC wellness claims — public claims grid, legal review of every page. Mindy Pelz competitive shadow — we lead with mental performance, never compete on hormonal cycles. Founder content burnout — eight weeks of video pre-recorded, bench of teachers in Phase 2. We wrote them down so you didn't have to ask."

**Cue:** Speak slowly here. The point is composure. Investors are buying *judgment* on this slide.

### Slide 11 — Vision · the quote *(0:20)*
> "Soma wins if, eighteen months from now, a Stanford researcher publishes a paper using Soma data, and a biohacker on a podcast says: *'I fast by the moon now — there's this app built by Nepali founders. It actually works.'* We are raising five hundred thousand to one and a half million to make that sentence true. Thank you."

**Cue:** Do not rush the quote. Let it sit. Then the *thank you*. Stop talking.

## B. Adversarial prep — where judges will attack

The structure: **the question** · **what they are really asking** · **the one-line counter** · **the deeper backup if they push**.

### B1. "Cultural authenticity isn't defensible — anyone can hire a Nepali advisor."
- **Real question:** Is the moat fake?
- **Counter:** "Hiring is a marketing line. Lineage is a biography. Our founder grew up doing this — that produces a content cadence and a tone of voice nobody can replicate from a job posting. The proof is whether the diaspora protects us, and we are seeding Wave 1 in five South Asian communities specifically for that reason."
- **Backup:** Cite that the marketing strategy treats founder voice as a permanent rule (§9.3), not a marketing tactic.

### B2. "Mindy Pelz is going to crush you."
- **Real question:** Have you thought about your most obvious competitor?
- **Counter:** "Mindy Pelz owns women's hormonal-cycle fasting. We are explicitly not in that lane. We lead with male-inclusive cognition — a quadrant she does not own and would dilute her brand to enter. If she ships a competing product we will know within a month and our differentiation gets *sharper*, not weaker."
- **Backup:** PRD §10 risk row · marketing strategy §2 competitive matrix.

### B3. "The Western science for lunar effects is thin. Aren't you over-claiming?"
- **Real question:** Will the FTC kill you?
- **Counter:** "Yes, the science is thin and we say so on the marketing page. Cajochen 2013 is real but narrow. The Calcutta diabetic study is n=42. That is exactly why we ship with a public claims grid — *associated with*, not *causes*; *users report*, not *clinically proven*. We celebrate the safety gates instead of hiding them. Trust is the brand."
- **Backup:** marketing strategy §9, allowed/forbidden language table.

### B4. "How do Nepali founders crack US distribution?"
- **Real question:** Is the GTM real or wishful?
- **Counter:** "We don't crack it. We earn it. Founder long-form content is the cheapest, highest-credibility channel for a niche brand and it compounds. Tier-2 podcasts (Modern Wisdom, Found My Fitness, The Knowledge Project) are realistic in Wave 2, and Tier-1 — Huberman, Attia — becomes accessible in Wave 3 once we have a data story they actually want. If after six months that engine isn't producing, the venture stops at Gate 3. We don't paper over it with paid."
- **Backup:** Marketing §7 channel ranking · biz §11.2 capture risk.

### B5. "The data-licensing line is unproven. You're banking on it."
- **Real question:** Is your business model dependent on a fantasy?
- **Counter:** "It is the upside, not the base case. Subscription has to be self-sustaining without it — that is written into the PRD risk register. Oura's research partnerships are the precedent we point to, but we sized SOM excluding licensing on purpose. Licensing turns an eight-million ARR niche into a twenty-five-million one. We are investable as the niche."
- **Backup:** biz §10 SOM table · PRD §10 row "Data licensing path doesn't materialize."

### B6. "Why web first instead of iOS? Your persona is on iPhone."
- **Real question:** Are you slow?
- **Counter:** "Speed of iteration. The web MVP exists *only* to validate adherence — to get fifty to two hundred beta users completing lunar cycles before we invest a quarter in HealthKit, Live Activities, and App Store review. iOS ships Phase 2, with HealthKit and Dynamic Island as launch features. The web MVP is a learning instrument, not the product."
- **Backup:** PRD §5 rationale · §6 mobile scope.

### B7. "What if beta adherence is below 40%?"
- **Real question:** Do you have the discipline to kill your darling?
- **Counter:** "We stop. Hard Gate-2 stop. No paid tier, no public launch, no spend. We rework the protocol or the persona, and we re-run the beta. Wellness apps die from premature scaling, not from slow validation. The gates exist so we don't die that way."
- **Backup:** PRD §7 phased gates · biz §12.

### B8. "Is the founder really willing to be on camera every week for 18 months?"
- **Real question:** Is the load-bearing assumption actually load-bearing?
- **Counter:** "Yes, and we know the strategy collapses without it. We pre-record eight weeks of founder video before launch as a buffer. We recruit two more Nepali teacher voices in Phase 2 to de-risk the key-person dependency. And we have a content editor budgeted from Wave 2 onward so the founder writes, but doesn't *publish*, alone."
- **Backup:** Marketing §11 Q1 · §12 risk row · §13 budget line.

### B9. "Your TAM is too small for venture returns."
- **Real question:** Are you the wrong shape for venture money?
- **Counter:** "Correct, for a Series A. We are not raising one. We are raising five hundred thousand to one and a half million in angel and small seed. The math works as a lifestyle business at five million ARR and as an acquisition target at twenty. If you are looking for a hundred-million-ARR consumer app, this is not the round. If you want a high-credibility niche brand with a real data moat that compounds, this is the round."
- **Backup:** biz §10, §14 fundraising implication.

### B10. "What stops Apple from shipping 'lunar phase' as a native Health feature?"
- **Real question:** Are you a feature, not a company?
- **Counter:** "Apple ships the *signal*. We ship the *protocol*. A lunar-phase widget is a calendar entry. Soma is a fasting schedule, a paired meditation library, an explainer layer, a community fast, and a research data pipeline. Apple has never built a behavior change product — they build APIs. If anything, an Apple lunar widget would *legitimize* the category and become a top-of-funnel asset for us."
- **Backup:** biz §3 SWOT threats · marketing §2 competitive narrative.

### B11. "What's your engineering team look like?" *(if asked)*
- **Honest answer:** "Engineering is the open question we are using this round to close. Web MVP scope is deliberately narrow — Next.js on Vercel, Postgres, Web Push, no native HealthKit — so it is buildable by a two-person team in three months. The PRD is explicit that we scope mobile only after Gate 2."

### B12. "Why now versus 12 months from now?"
- **Counter:** "Two reasons. One: cultural authenticity is the consumer signal of 2026, and that window does not stay open if a Western brand co-opts the framing first. Two: every month we delay is a month of consented data we don't have, and the data moat compounds — month one of data is worth less than month eighteen of data. The cost of waiting is paid in defensibility."

## C. Self-check · what to know before you walk on stage

These are the things *we already know are weak* about the deck, so you are not surprised in the room.

### C1. Numbers that are honest estimates, not measured truth
| Number | Source | Risk if challenged |
|---|---|---|
| LTV $180 / 2-year retention | Aggressive estimate · biz §11.3 | Wellness D365 is typically <30%. If asked: "Aggressive — based on cohort cohesion from cultural-ritual products, not on a measured Soma cohort. We will know the real number after Gate 2." |
| Free→paid 8–12% | Industry-typical for niche premium | Honest answer: "Industry typical for niche premium wellness; not yet measured for Soma." |
| SOM $3–8M ARR | Top-down sizing | "Order of magnitude — we will tighten with primary research in Wave 1." |
| Cajochen 2013 / Calcutta n=42 | Real but narrow | Volunteer the limitation before they ask. It is a *trust signal*. |

**Rule:** If you don't know a number, say so out loud and name when you'll know it. Never guess. Investors forgive ignorance, they do not forgive bluffing.

### C2. Open questions still unresolved (PRD §11 / context §8)
You should be ready to say *"we have not decided yet"* on:
1. Face of Soma — founder solo, hired teacher, or hybrid.
2. Gender framing — neutral cognition first, female-cycle layered later (current recommendation).
3. Phase 1 north star — LCAR vs. focus delta.
4. Wearable priority at MVP — Apple Health only (current plan) vs. Whoop/Oura earlier.
5. Chandrayana mode — Phase 2 paid with attestation, or removed entirely.
6. Legal wrapper for the dataset — for-profit, nonprofit, or data trust.

If a judge picks one of these, the right move is: **"We have not decided. Here is what each option costs us. Here is when we will decide."** Show the trade-off matrix in your head, not a confident wrong answer.

### C3. Things deliberately *not* in the deck (and why)
- **Detailed competitive teardown of Mindy Pelz** — would draw the deck into her gravity. The competitor map handles it visually.
- **Detailed financial model** — premature for a five-minute pitch. Have it as a follow-up PDF.
- **Founder bio slide** — the founder *is* the bio. Read it in the room, not on a slide. If asked, you have 30 seconds of personal story rehearsed.
- **Team slide** — Engineering is unresolved (see B11). Don't promise what isn't true.
- **Detailed product feature list** — already implied by the phone mockup. Adding F1–F15 dilutes the wedge.

### C4. Things the deck *over*-promises and you should soften live
- The flywheel slide makes data licensing look inevitable. **Soften:** "This is the upside arc. The base case works without it."
- The roadmap slide makes the gates look automatic. **Soften:** "Each gate is a real stop. We have written down what we do if we fail one — pause spending, rework, re-test."
- The market slide makes the niche look glamorous. **Soften:** "We are not pretending this is a unicorn. We are pretending this is a credible niche brand with one rare upside lever."

### C5. Body language and pacing
- **Hands:** out of pockets. Use them for the flywheel slide and the lunar timeline.
- **Pacing:** the deck has two paper slides and three ink slides — the visual rhythm tells the audience when you are shifting gears. Match your tone to the slide background. Paper = clarity. Ink = stakes.
- **Eye contact:** during Slide 6 (Moat) and Slide 11 (Vision), look at the audience, not the slide. These are the two slides that decide the room.
- **Pause after the vision quote.** Resist the urge to fill the silence. Let the room sit with it for one full beat before you say *thank you*.

### C6. The single sentence to fall back on if the room goes cold
> *"Soma is built by people whose grandparents fasted by the moon — and we are translating a thousand-year-old protocol for the modern mind, with the data to prove it works."*

This is the line from marketing strategy §0. It is the one sentence you can say at any point and not regret.

## D. Logistics

### D1. Deck operation
- File: `pitch.html` at the project root. Open in any modern browser, full-screen with `Cmd+Ctrl+F` (Mac) or `F11` (Windows).
- Navigation: `→` / `←` arrows · `space` / `PageDown` advances · number keys `1–9` jump · `0` jumps to slide 10 · click anywhere also advances.
- The deck is fully self-contained except for two CDN dependencies (Tailwind, Google Fonts). **For an offline room, open it once on the machine before the pitch so the CDN assets cache.**
- Aspect ratio: it fills the viewport. Use 16:9. Test on the venue projector at least 30 minutes before the pitch.

### D2. Fallback if the deck won't load
- Have a PDF export ready (`Cmd+P` → Save as PDF, landscape, no margins). Print one copy as a backup paper deck.
- Have one slide of "key numbers" memorized: 1,000 years · 6 fasts/cycle · LCAR ≥ 40% · $99 ARPU · 75% margin · CAC < $30 · LTV/CAC 6:1 · $0.5–1.5M ask.

### D3. Q&A budget
A 5-minute pitch usually has 3–5 minutes of Q&A. That is 4–6 questions. Prep the answers in Section B in **the order judges actually ask them**, which is almost always:
1. Distribution / GTM (B4)
2. Competitive moat (B1 or B2)
3. Market size or business model (B5 or B9)
4. Risk you're underestimating (B3, B7, or B8)

Practice the first sentence of each answer. If you nail the first sentence, the rest follows.

## E. The one-line tests for the whole pitch

Before you walk on stage, ask yourself:

1. **Does my five-minute story make all three of these believable?** → founder authenticity · ancient protocol · measurable mental performance.
2. **Have I said one thing the room did not already know?** → the data flywheel + the IRB-grade consent from day one.
3. **Have I said one thing that makes the room trust me more than they trust the deck?** → the gates. The honest size. The risks slide. The "we don't know" answers.
4. **Have I closed with the sentence I want them quoting in the hallway?** → *"Built by people whose grandparents fasted by the moon."*

If yes to all four, you have done the job. The rest is the partner conversation — and that is a different document.
